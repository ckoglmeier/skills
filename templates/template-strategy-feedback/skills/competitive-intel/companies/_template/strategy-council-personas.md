# Strategy Council Personas: [COMPANY NAME]

> Last Updated: [DATE]
> Updated By: [NAME/TEAM]

Define 8-12 personas for the Strategy Council. These represent the perspectives that matter most when making product, pricing, investment, and strategic decisions at your company.

**Recommended persona types to include:**
- An early-stage investor voice (market creation, defensibility)
- A late-stage investor voice (revenue quality, category leadership)
- A CRO / commercial leader (GTM reality, sales motion)
- A brand / narrative voice (positioning, messaging clarity)
- A product design voice (UX, user experience reality)
- A customer success / delivery voice (implementation, scale)
- Your primary buyer persona (how buyers actually decide)
- A domain expert relevant to your industry (e.g., academic partner, clinical leader, regulatory)
- A learner / end-user advocate (the person who actually uses the product)
- A CFO / finance voice (margin, unit economics, capital allocation)
- A technical / engineering voice (buildability, AI, infrastructure)
- A regulatory / compliance voice (if relevant to your industry)

Not every persona speaks on every question. The council selects 3-5 relevant voices per question.

## QUERY TEMPLATES

Customize these for your company's common decision types:

**New Product Validation:**
"Council: [product concept]. 1) Does this solve a real problem? 2) What's the business model and who pays? 3) Who else could build this — why is this ours to own? 4) What breaks at scale? 5) Would [buyer persona] buy it, and would [end user] use it?"

**Pricing:**
"Council: [pricing model or change]. 1) Can the buyer justify this to their CFO? 2) What's the payback story? 3) How does this affect partner/vendor economics? 4) What does this pricing signal about our positioning?"

**Product Enhancement:**
"Council: [enhancement]. 1) Does this serve the user, the buyer, or both? 2) What's the implementation reality? 3) Does this differentiate or just add surface area?"

**GTM Strategy:**
"[CRO name], [CS name]: [GTM plan]. 1) Where's the conversion bottleneck? 2) Can sales execute this? 3) Can delivery keep up? 4) What breaks at 10x?"
