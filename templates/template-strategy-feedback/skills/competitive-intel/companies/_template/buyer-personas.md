Define your buyer personas and internal commercial voices. These represent how your buyers actually think, decide, and react to messaging, campaigns, and pitches.

**Recommended persona types:**

*External Buyer Voices (how your buyers actually decide):*
- 2-4 variations of your primary buyer by industry, company size, or segment
- The budget owner / financial gatekeeper
- The end-user advocate or champion
- The operational leader who has to live with the implementation
- The CFO / financial skeptic

*Internal Voices (your company's commercial lens):*
- CRO / Head of Sales (deal reality, conversion, multi-threading)
- CMO / Marketing leader (brand, channels, messaging)
- End-user advocate / customer-facing role (the person closest to the user)

## QUERY TEMPLATES

**Messaging Review (Single Persona):**
"Persona council: [copy or message]. How does this land for [Name]? 1) What resonates? 2) What's missing? 3) What objection isn't pre-empted? 4) Rewrite the opening line."

**Multi-Persona Messaging:**
"Persona council: [campaign or message]. 1) Does this land at the right altitude for each buyer? 2) Where does it fall flat? 3) Is there a throughline? 4) Is this differentiated from [competitor]?"

**Outbound Campaign:**
"Persona council: [outbound — target, channel, message]. 1) What's the hook? 2) What channel reaches them? 3) How do we sequence across buyers in one account? 4) What proof point moves each one?"

**Client Pitch Prep:**
"Persona council: [client profile]. 1) Who's in the room and what do they care about? 2) Where does this deal stall? 3) What's the opening hook per buyer? 4) What kills this deal?"

**Competitive Displacement:**
"Persona council: [competitor situation]. 1) How does each persona perceive the incumbent? 2) Where are we stronger — and where vulnerable? 3) What's the counter-narrative?"
