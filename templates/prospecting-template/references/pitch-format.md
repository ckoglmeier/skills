# Pitch Hook Format

## The Rule

Every pitch hook is exactly 2 sentences:

- **Sentence 1:** References something specific the person has said or done publicly. Not general industry trends — their words, their company's actions, their specific mandate. If you can't find a real signal, write "no public signal found" and do not fabricate.
- **Sentence 2:** Connects their specific context to what your product does for them. One concrete outcome, not a capabilities list.

Never use: "exciting," "love what you're building," "transformative," "cutting-edge," or any generic language that could apply to anyone.

## Format

```
[Specific thing they said/did that creates the problem or tension]. [Your product] [specific outcome that resolves it] — [one concrete detail that makes it real].
```

## Good Example Structure

**What works:**

> You said [EXACT QUOTE OR SPECIFIC ACTION] — [PRODUCT] [SPECIFIC OUTCOME RELEVANT TO THAT QUOTE], so [CONCRETE BUSINESS RESULT they care about].

*Why it works: References their exact words or a specific event. Names their specific context. Turns their stated urgency into the pitch.*

---

> You just [SPECIFIC COMPANY EVENT — funding, acquisition, product launch, new role] — [PRODUCT] [WHAT IT DOES FOR THAT SPECIFIC SITUATION], before [THE DECISION OR DEADLINE THEY'RE FACING].

*Why it works: Ties timing trigger to a decision already being made. Positions your product as an input to something they're actively working on, not an interruption.*

---

> You're [SPECIFIC MANDATE OR CHALLENGE from their role/appointment] — [PRODUCT] gives you [SPECIFIC OUTPUT] that makes [THEIR MANDATE] specific and measurable, before you're [WHAT HAPPENS WITHOUT IT].

*Why it works: Uses their exact mandate language. Creates urgency around a real consequence of not acting.*

## Bad Examples — Do Not Write Like This

❌ "Your work in [SPACE] is impressive — [PRODUCT] helps companies like yours identify opportunities across their workflows."

*Why it fails: Generic. Could apply to anyone. "Impressive" is filler.*

---

❌ "As someone leading [FUNCTION] at a rapidly growing company, you understand the challenges of [PROBLEM]. [PRODUCT] solves that problem for you."

*Why it fails: Three generic claims. "Rapidly growing" means nothing. No specific reference to anything real.*

---

❌ "We'd love to connect and share how [PRODUCT] can help [COMPANY] with [GENERIC OUTCOME]. We've worked with similar companies and the results have been transformative."

*Why it fails: Template language. "Transformative." No specifics. This is every other outbound email in their inbox.*

## For Warm Outreach (Direct Connections)

When reaching out to someone you know, tone shifts slightly:
- Acknowledge the relationship briefly but don't lean on it too hard
- Still lead with their specific context — shared history doesn't replace a real pitch
- Slightly more casual sentence structure is fine
- A closing question ("worth a quick call?") is appropriate; skip it for cold outreach
